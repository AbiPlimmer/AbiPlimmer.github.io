About

I am a PhD researcher at Cardiff University, funded by the NERC GW4+ Doctoral Training Partnership (DTP). My research focuses on the evolution of the Earth's interior, utilising numerical modelling to investigate the interactions between mantle geodynamics and tectonic processes, and how these relationships shift throughout the supercontinent cycle. I am especially interested in how slabs and plumes connect the shallow and deep Earth, driving the dynamics of the mantle circulation system. In my work, I primarily use and develop the 3D mantle convection code TERRA to simulate Earth's long-term evolution, with a focus on the most recent supercontinent cycle.

This pursuit builds upon the numerical modelling experience gained through my Masters studies at the University of Leeds, where my dissertation married 2D models (using the ASPECT code) with seismic data to investigate the controls on magamtism at rifted margins.
I am eager to further refine my numerical modelling skills to continue exploring these research questions, and I will soon be seeking post-doctoral opportunities. Please feel free to reach out if you're interested in collaborations, future projects, or simply to discuss research ideas!
